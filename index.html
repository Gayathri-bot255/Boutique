1. Saree

A saree is a traditional Indian garment consisting of a single, unstitched piece of fabric, typically 5 to 9 yards long, draped elegantly around the body. It is paired with a fitted blouse and a petticoat, showcasing regional weaves, patterns, and craftsmanship, symbolizing grace and cultural heritage.
  1. Pre-draped pleats for effortless saree styling
  2. Embellished borders with zari, sequins, or embroidery
  3. Comes with a detachable dupatta or pallu
 Weddings, receptions, festive events RMKV
2. Lehenga

A lehenga is a traditional Indian ensemble comprising a long, flared skirt, paired with a fitted blouse (choli) and a dupatta (scarf). Often adorned with intricate embroidery, sequins, or mirror work, it is a popular choice for weddings and festive occasions, symbolizing elegance and grandeur.
  1. A flared skirt with heavy embroidery or mirror work
  2. Includes a lightweight dupatta for added elegance
  3. Paired with a crop top or embellished blouse
 Engagements, traditional functions Myntra
3. Palazzo Kurta

A Palazzo Kurta is a stylish and versatile Indian outfit featuring a long kurta paired with wide-leg palazzo pants. Combining comfort and elegance, it often showcases intricate prints or embroidery, making it a popular choice for casual wear, office attire, or festive occasions.
  1. Straight-cut or A-line kurta paired with wide-leg palazzos
  2. Features subtle embroidery or bold prints
  3. Includes pockets for added functionality
 Office wear, casual gatherings Libas
4. Chanderi Midi

A Chanderi midi is a knee-length or calf-length dress made from Chanderi silk, known for its lightweight and breathable texture. It features intricate zari or gold-thread motifs, offering a blend of elegance and comfort, making it a perfect choice for festive or daytime events. The fabric's subtle sheen adds a touch of sophistication to the outfit.
  1. Made from Chanderi silk with golden zari motifs
  2. Knee-length or calf-length dress with pleated detailing
  3. Lightweight and breathable for summer
 Festive daytime events, temple visits Chandrima
